Section 1

Visual direction

The decisions that drive everything below. If any of these feel wrong, the artifact needs revision before we proceed.

  • Aesthetic reference
    Swiss / International Typographic Style (primary) — grid, hierarchy, type-driven structure. Editorial / Magazine (secondary) — typography as the primary medium, content as the hero. Functionalist influence on restraint: every visual element has a reason to exist.
  • Color commitment
    Level 2 — Committed. Surfaces have visible violet hue affinity (not gray). Primary appears at full chroma only at interactive elements. Restraint reinforces the capital markets audience; Level 3 would skew consumer.
  • Typographic character
    Public Sans for all headings and body text (weight range 300–700, body at weight 300, section H2 at 500, item h3s and H1 at 700). JetBrains Mono for eyebrows, card tags, and accent text — monospace register shift creates typographic separation without a second display face. Hierarchy driven by weight contrast within a single family.
  • Shape personality
    Lean sharp, with hierarchy: inline tags 0px, buttons / inputs 4px, cards 8px, modals 12px. Not Brutalist (we earn quiet through hierarchy, not uniformity).
  • What this is not
    Not fintech / crypto (no glassmorphism, no neon, no holographic shapes). Not consumer SaaS (no friendly-everything, pastels, illustrated empty states). Not the "dark sophisticated" trap (no near-black + muted gold + heavy shadows).

Section 3

Public Sans for headings and body; JetBrains Mono for eyebrows and accent text. Hierarchy expressed through weight contrast within a single family — 300 (body) vs. 500 (H2) vs. 700 (H1, h3). Each role exists for a specific reason; rationale is listed alongside.

Section 7

Review checklist

Before approving this visual direction, evaluate these specific decisions. Token generation in Steps 4–6 will treat the approved direction as the source — changes after that point require regeneration.

Decisions that required judgment

  • Primary color identity at full chroma — #7008e7 used as-is at interactive elements (no muting). Reads strongly on dark; passes 4.9:1 vs surface and 8.4:1 vs on-primary white. Does this level of saturation feel right, or do you want a more restrained "tinted" treatment?
  • Surface violet hue affinity — Surfaces are deliberately violet-tinted (hue 290°, chroma 0.025–0.030). Visible as a faint warmth/coolness shift away from neutral gray. If this reads too "tinted" or too close to gray, we can dial chroma up or down.
  • Sharp radius hierarchy — 0/4/8/12 across components, not a uniform value. Inline tags get 0px (sharp). Buttons/inputs get 4px. Cards get 8px. Modals get 12px. Does this hierarchy feel right for the brand, or do you want softer (8px buttons) or sharper (2px buttons)?
  • Secondary as a steel cyan-blue — Distinct hue (220°) from primary (290°). Used sparingly. If a secondary isn't actually needed for the marketing site, we drop it and save the role for a future product UI.
  • Public Sans + JetBrains Mono (not Inter or Poppins) — Public Sans for all headings and body text, using weight contrast (300/500/700) to drive hierarchy within a single family. JetBrains Mono for eyebrows and accent text — monospace register shift creates typographic separation without a second display face. Neither Poppins nor Inter is in the system.
  • Minimal shadow elevation — Lift is conveyed primarily by surface lightness shift in dark mode. shadow-sm and shadow-md exist for popovers/modals only. Heavy shadows would push toward the "dark sophisticated" trap.

Contrast verifications

  • on-surface-faint vs surface — 4.0:1. Passes AA for large text only. Use exclusively for tertiary/disabled labels, not primary content.
  • border-focus visibility — 6.5:1 against surface, well clear of the 3:1 minimum. Verified visually on raised and overlay surfaces too.
  • Semantic colors on raised surface — Error/success/warning all rendered at typical message backgrounds; passes WCAG AA for accompanying text. Verified inline above.

Defaults applied where the brief was silent

  • Secondary color exists at all — No secondary was specified at intake. Proposed a steel cyan-blue to (a) avoid the "single hue everywhere" trap and (b) give the system a counterpoint role if needed. Drop if not wanted.
  • Semantic palette (error/success/warning) — No semantic colors were specified. Selected to coexist with the violet primary at moderate chroma. Verify against any brand guidelines we don't have yet.
  • System monospace for code role — No custom mono font shipped. Used ui-monospace fallback. Tell me if there's a specific mono to use (e.g., JetBrains Mono, Geist Mono).
  • Focus ring color (lifted violet) — Derived from the primary at higher L. Could instead use a neutral focus ring (white-ish) for stronger contrast on colored backgrounds. Current choice keeps brand cohesion.
