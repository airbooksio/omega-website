Investors

Building the foundational data layer for fixed-income capital markets.

We're replacing the bundled, monopolistic infrastructure that's held fixed-income markets back for two decades — with modern workflow software and an open, governed protocol underneath. The wedge: the $500B U.S. municipal primary market. The expansion path: the rest of fixed income, then the agentic-finance era.

The thesis

A wedge into a category nobody has been able to disrupt.

The problem is structural.

Fixed-income capital markets run on infrastructure bundled by a single dominant vendor. Lock-in is the default. Internal builds can't escape it because the network — not the application — is the moat.

The opening is now.

AI, agentic workflows, and tokenization need clean, governed, accessible market data. The current stack can't deliver it. The market is actively looking for an alternative for the first time in 20 years.

The wedge is muni primary.

A $500B market with universal acknowledgment that the status quo is broken — and where Airbooks already has products in active deployment. Once muni works, the playbook extends directly to corporate IG, high yield, and private credit.

The team is the right one.

Capital markets veterans who've run the desks, built the tools, and consulted on this exact problem for over a decade — paired with engineering leadership in protocol and standards-based systems.

Traction

Real products, real partners.

Products in market

Postgame, Thunderwriter Pro, the Buyside Suite, and the Sync Bridge are deployed or production-ready today. The OMEGA protocol layer underneath is in initial deployment.

Active engagement

Active deployment and partnership conversations underway with top-tier broker-dealers and a leading electronic trading platform in the municipal market.

Growing pipeline

Sustained inbound interest across sell-side and buy-side firms in the municipal market, with adjacent fixed-income asset classes already requesting roadmap conversations.

Market opportunity

Beachhead → expansion.

Fixed-income capital markets infrastructure is a multi-billion dollar category bundled into a single dominant vendor. The wedge is muni primary because the pain is most acute, the players are concentrated, and the workflows are well-understood.

From muni primary, the same protocol and the same workflow patterns extend into corporate IG, high yield, securitized products, and private credit. Each is a multiple of the muni TAM. The longer-term opportunity — clean, governed market data as the substrate for agentic and tokenized finance — is larger still.

[ Diagram: TAM expansion — muni primary → corporate IG → HY → private credit → broader fixed income ]
The longer-term vision

The governed layer for agentic finance.

AI agents and autonomous workflows can't operate on data that's locked, proprietary, and ungoverned. OMEGA is the substrate that makes them possible — clean schemas, real governance, programmatic access. This is the bigger story underneath the muni wedge.
