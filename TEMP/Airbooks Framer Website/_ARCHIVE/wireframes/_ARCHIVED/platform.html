How OMEGA works

Exchange. Governance. Access.

Three layers that together replace the bundled, monolithic infrastructure capital markets has been forced to live with.

Exchange

An API-first data and messaging layer with common contracts for capital markets data. Connect any system, ingest from any source, and route messages between participants without proprietary lock-in.

Governance

Federated, policy-as-code rules — designed to be operated by a non-profit consortium of market participants. Default policies are public, auditable, and contributable. Shared rules, individual control.

Access

Zero-trust, participant-managed access controls. Every participant decides who sees their data and at what granularity. No proprietary formats, no exit fees, no surprise resale.

For developers and engineering teams

Open by default, integrate by design.

OpenAPI v3.1

Every OMEGA endpoint is documented in OpenAPI. Generate client libraries in any language, mock against the spec, and validate contracts automatically.

Policy-as-code

Governance rules expressed in OPA / Rego. Audit, simulate, and propose changes without lawyers in the loop.

Build your own apps

Internal teams can build directly on OMEGA — same protocol, same data contracts, same governance — alongside or in place of Airbooks's own applications.

Governance model

Run by the market, not by Airbooks.

OMEGA's governance is structured to migrate to a non-profit consortium of market participants. Airbooks operates and contributes to the protocol — but doesn't own it. The point isn't to swap one walled garden for another. It's to give the market a layer it can actually trust.

Default policies are public. Proposed changes go through an open process. Every participant has a clear path to inspect, contest, or contribute.
