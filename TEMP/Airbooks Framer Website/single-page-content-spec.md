# 3. Section: The Problem

**In-page anchor:** `#problem`

**Eyebrow:**
> The Problem

**Section header (H2):**
> Capital markets infrastructure is gated, bundled, and built for a different era.

**Subheader / intro paragraph:**
> Capital markets teams have two options today: rent workflows from a single entrenched vendor, or build internally and watch software without market connectivity fail to gain traction. Neither is working.

**Layout:** 3 cards, horizontal grid.

### Card 1
- **Card title:** Gated by a single vendor.
- **Card body:** The incumbent bundles aging applications with the network layer the market depends on. Either you take both, or you don't get connected.

### Card 2
- **Card title:** You can't build your own way in.
- **Card body:** The largest firms have built their own tools. Software without market connectivity is an island.

### Card 3
- **Card title:** Your own data is sold back to you.
- **Card body:** Transaction data is surrendered to a single vendor and resold back as analytics.

# 5. Section: The Open Platform (OMEGA)

**In-page anchor:** `#platform`

**Eyebrow:**
> The Open Platform

**Section header (H2):**
> An open access layer, by design.

**Subheader / intro paragraph:**
> Behind every Airbooks application is OMEGA — the Open Markets Exchange, Governance & Access protocol. Access is in the name for a reason.

**Layout:** 3 cards, horizontal grid.

### Card 1
- **Card title:** You control who gets access to your data.
- **Card body:** Zero-trust by default. Granular, participant-managed access controls. No proprietary formats, no exit fees, no analytics built from your own data and sold back to you.

### Card 2
- **Card title:** Built for interoperability.
- **Card body:** API-first, mapped to common standards (ICMA, CDM, DTCC, MSRB). Connect any system, ingest from any source. Third parties — including your own teams — can build on top.

### Card 3
- **Card title:** Governed by the market, not a vendor.
- **Card body:** OMEGA's governance layer is designed to be run by a non-profit consortium of market participants, with default policies open to all. Shared rules, individual control, no single point of capture.

**Graphics:** None.
